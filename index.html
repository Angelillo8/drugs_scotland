Drug-related deaths, heroin, benzodiazepines and Harm Reduction in Scotland

Introduction

Abstract

Scotland leads all EU countries in the number of drug-related deaths. Other substances such as benzodiazepines and street benzos, not to mention the notorious heroin, contribute to this figure, together accounting for almost 80 per cent of drug-related deaths. Despite this, the Scottish government pursues a policy of "harm reduction" towards drugs, a stance aimed at destigmatising the substances known as drugs and their use. However, substances such as heroin generate a strong dependency for the user, making it difficult to consider any kind of responsible recreational use; considering also the wide range of side effects that heroin and benzodiazepines generate in the short and long term. Is this approach of the Scottish Government the most adequate for these substances?
